About PowerWatch

Why this exists, and what it solves.

The problem

Power cuts in many localities are frequent but completely undocumented. When the power goes out, residents can't easily tell if it's just their house or the whole street. Nobody tracks which areas or times see the most outages, so patterns that are obvious over months stay invisible day to day. And without any record, there's nothing concrete to bring to the electricity board when it's time to complain.

How PowerWatch helps

  • Real-time confirmation — if several people in the same area log an outage within minutes, everyone knows instantly it isn't just them.
  • Pattern visibility — the Statistics page turns scattered individual reports into visible trends by area and day.
  • Accountability data — a simple, concrete record to bring to the people responsible for fixing it.

A note on this prototype

This version saves data in your browser's LocalStorage, so it's fully functional but only visible on your own device — it's a working proof of concept, not yet a shared community tool. A real deployment would need a shared backend (for example Firebase, or a small Node.js service with a database) so that everyone in a locality sees the same live feed and statistics.
